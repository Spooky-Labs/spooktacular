Privacy Policy

Effective 2026-04-19 · Short version: Spooktacular is local-first and doesn't phone home.

Plain-language summary. Spooktacular runs entirely on your Mac. It makes no analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting connections to Spooky Labs. The only outbound connections are to services you explicitly configure or invoke (Apple, GitHub, your own observability endpoints). There is no account, no sign-in, no user identifier.

1. Who we are

Spooky Labs ("we", "us") publishes Spooktacular ("the app"). You can reach us at spook@spookylabs.ai. The app is open source (MIT) — anything we claim here is verifiable in the repository.

2. Data the app does not collect or transmit

  • We do not collect analytics, usage metrics, or telemetry.
  • We do not collect crash reports automatically (macOS's opt-in Apple diagnostics are Apple's mechanism, not ours).
  • We do not collect your IP address, device identifier, advertising ID, or any other cross-app identifier.
  • We do not assign you an account, user ID, or anonymous identifier.
  • We do not track which VMs you run, what's inside them, or what the spook CLI is used for.
  • We do not read or transmit the contents of your VMs, your guest OS, or any file on your Mac.

3. Outbound connections the app may make

The app only initiates the network requests you ask it to make. In practice, those are:

  • Apple (mesu.apple.com, appldnld.apple.com) — for macOS IPSW restore-image metadata and download, when you run spook create with the default source. Skip entirely by passing --from-ipsw <local path>.
  • GitHub (api.github.com, objects.githubusercontent.com) — when you create a VM from the GitHub Actions runner template, to download the runner binary and register it with your repository.
  • Your Kubernetes API server, S3 bucket, or Prometheus/Grafana endpoint — when you explicitly configure Spooktacular to integrate with them.
  • Your IdP (OIDC / SAML) — when multi-tenant authentication is enabled.

All outbound connections to third parties are initiated by your configuration, not by us. None of those endpoints are Spooky Labs.

4. Data the app stores locally on your Mac

  • VM bundles under ~/.spooktacular/vms/ — the guest disk images, config, and metadata you create.
  • IPSW cache under ~/.spooktacular/ipsw/ — downloaded Apple restore images, content-addressed by SHA-256.
  • Audit log (if enabled) under ~/Library/Logs/Spooktacular/ — append-only JSONL records of security decisions.
  • Keychain items under service names com.spooktacular.* — tokens you explicitly add via the security CLI.
  • SwiftUI state via NSUserDefaults under com.spooktacular.app — window sizes, last-opened VMs, and UI preferences.

All of this stays on your Mac unless you explicitly copy it elsewhere.

5. Audit logs & SIEM export (opt-in)

If you enable the audit subsystem, Spooktacular writes signed, hash-chained records to a local JSONL file and, optionally, to an S3 bucket or SIEM webhook that you configure. The destination is entirely yours — we do not receive a copy. See docs/AUDIT_STATUS.md in the repo.

6. OpenTelemetry / Prometheus (opt-in)

If you set SPOOK_OTLP_ENDPOINT or point the Helm chart's ServiceMonitor at an observability stack, Spooktacular emits traces and metrics to that endpoint. The endpoint is yours. We don't see the data.

7. The Mac App Store build

When you install Spooktacular from the Mac App Store, Apple collects App Store telemetry under its own privacy terms. That's Apple, not us; see Apple's privacy policy. We don't receive per-install or usage analytics from Apple.

8. Children

Spooktacular is a developer tool and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone, children included.

9. Security

Local data is protected by macOS file system permissions; where the host is a portable Mac, Spooktacular sets the bundle's FileProtectionType so VMs are encrypted at rest when the laptop is powered off. Secrets live in the Keychain with ACLs. The Security page has the full posture.

10. Your rights

Because we don't collect personal data from you, there is nothing for us to delete, export, rectify, or transfer. If you're in a jurisdiction that grants a specific statutory right (GDPR, CCPA, others) and you believe we hold data about you, email privacy@spookylabs.ai and we'll investigate — but the honest default answer is "we have nothing."

11. Changes to this policy

If we materially change what the app does — for example, if we ever added an opt-in telemetry feature — we would update this page and, for users with the app installed, surface the change in the app's What's New. We'd never flip previously-private behavior on by default.

12. Contact

Privacy questions: privacy@spookylabs.ai. General: spook@spookylabs.ai. Security disclosures: security@spookylabs.ai (see SECURITY.md for the PGP key).
